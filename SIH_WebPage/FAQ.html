Frequently Asked Questions

Smart Health Surveillance and Early Warning System

FAQs

1. What is the purpose of this system?

The system helps detect early signs of water-borne and seasonal diseases in rural areas. By collecting community reports and analyzing them with AI, it sends alerts before outbreaks spread.

2. Who can use this platform?

Villagers, health workers, and government health officials can use the platform. Villagers report symptoms, health workers validate data, and officials receive alerts with hotspot details.

3. How are the alerts generated?

When many users from the same area report similar symptoms, the system identifies a hotspot and sends an SMS alert to the health department helpline.

4. Is my personal data safe?

Yes. All data is encrypted and only shared with authorized health officials. No personal data is sold or shared with third parties.

5. What diseases can this system detect?

The platform mainly focuses on water-borne diseases such as Cholera, Typhoid, Hepatitis A, and other common rural outbreaks like Dengue, Malaria, and Diarrheal diseases.

6. What should I do if I receive an alert?

If you receive an alert, follow health guidelines, contact your local health worker, and take preventive steps like drinking clean water, using mosquito nets, and seeking medical advice.
